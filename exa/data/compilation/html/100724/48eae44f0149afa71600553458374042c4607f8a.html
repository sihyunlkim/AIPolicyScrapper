OFFICE OF THE PROVOST & VICE PRESIDENT OF ACADEMIC AFFAIRS

MISSION, VISION, VALUES & STRATEGIC GOALS

MISSION
The mission of the Office of the Provost and Vice President for Academic Affairs is to promote academic excellence in a transformative learning environment to support the University’s commitment to global excellence in teaching, research, and service. 

VISION
The Office of the Provost and Vice President for Academic Affairs is committed to the advancement of students, faculty, and staff within and beyond university boundaries.

VALUES
In the Office of the Provost and Vice President for Academic Affairs, planning and decision-making are guided by the values of Academic Excellence, Research, Creative Activities, Collaboration, Stewardship, and Community.

STRATEGIC GOALS
The Office of the Provost and Vice President for Academic Affairs endeavors to advance the division mission, vision, and values through: 

  • Effective and flexible learning and support services;
  • Learning experiences that foster intellectual curiosity and lifelong learning;
  • Globalization of the institution’s academic footprint;
  • Sponsored research capacities for facilitating technology transfer from research discovery tocommercial development;
  • Support for teaching, learning, and research
  • Expanding access to education through various modes of learning
  • Excellence in scholarship and creative activity
  • Promotion of professional development for students, faculty, and staff
  • Academic programming responsive to market demands
  • Partnerships to promote student success and research
  • Prudent management of institutional resources
  • Data-driven operating processes; and
  • Community engagement at the local, regional, national, and global levels.

SACSCOC

Alabama State University is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges (SACSCOC) to award baccalaureate, masters, educational specialist, and doctorate. Alabama State University also may offer credentials such as certificates and diplomas at approved degree levels. Questions about the accreditation of Alabama State University may be directed in writing to the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges at 1866 Southern Lane, Decatur, GA 30033-4097, by calling (404) 679-4500, or by using information available on SACSCOC’s website (www.sacscoc.org).
