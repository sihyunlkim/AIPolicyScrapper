Frequently Asked Questions

We will provide regular updates and are happy to answer any additional questions that may not be addressed on this page.  If you need further assistance, feel free to contact us at conductofficer@aamu.edu . 

  • Where is the Conduct Office located?
    The Office of Student Conduct and Community Standards is temporarily located Patton Hall Suite 100. Back to Top
  • What is the best way to contact the Office of Student Conduct and Community Standards?

    The Office of Student Conduct and Community Standards may be reached by calling (256) 372-5233 or emailing conductofficer@aamu.edu.  

    Back to Top
  • To whom does the Student Code of Conduct apply and when does it apply?

    The Student Code of Conduct applies to all students enrolled in the University. The term “student” includes anyone admitted to the University and registered for, taking, or pursuing undergraduate, graduate, or professional studies or courses at the University, both full-time and part-time, as well as persons attending classes on campus or off-campus. Persons who are not officially enrolled for a particular term but who have a continuing relationship with the University as well as persons participating in orientation are considered “students.”

    Back to Top
  • Why does the University enforce Student Code of Conduct policies off campus?

    You are an AAMU student regardless of whether the alleged misconduct takes place on the campus, across the street from campus, or off campus. The University has a vital interest in the character of its students and, therefore, regards behavior at any location (on-campus or off-campus) as a reflection of a student’s character and fitness to be a member of the student body.

    Back to Top
  • How are incidents reported to the Office of Student Conduct and Community Standards?

    The Office of Student Conduct and Community Standards receives Conduct Referrals from faculty/staff, the Department of Public Safety, and referrals from individuals and University departments.

    Back to Top
  • My Conduct Case is still open and unresolved. What will happen?

    We are continuing to address open cases. Preliminary meetings and hearings are conducted within 5-7 business days of the incident report by phone and/or Zoom Video Conferences.

    Back to Top
  • My student received a preliminary meeting letter. What happens next?

    Any student who is contacted by the Office of Student Conduct and Community Standards is expected to attend their scheduled appointment with their assigned Hearing Officer on the date noted in the letter. During the meeting, the Hearing Officer will review the alleged violations, Code of Student Conduct and conduct process with the student. The student will have the opportunity to review the documents and share any additional information with the Hearing Officer. If the Hearing Officer finds there is enough information to support the conduct charges, then the student has the option of resolving through the informal or formal resolution process. Detailed information about the conduct process can be found in the Student Code of Conduct.

    Back to Top
  • What happens if I do not show up for my  meeting?

    If you fail to attend your scheduled meeting, a hold will be placed on your record and you will be unable to register for classes or make changes to your schedule.

    Back to Top
  • I have a question about my sanctions or conduct status. What do I do?

    Please email conductofficer@aamu.edu if you have a question about your sanctions or conduct status. Students may be provided an extension for sanction deadlines depending on the circumstance.  Continue to work diligently to complete your sanctions on time. Conduct Holds will not be lifted until ALL sanctions are completed.

    Back to Top
  • I have a Judicial Hold (JD) on my account. What should I do?

    If you have a hold on your account, it is most likely for one of the following reasons:

    • Failure to attend a scheduled meeting with your conduct hearing officer
    • Failure to complete the sanctions assigned to you.

    You will have to send an email to conductofficer@aamu.edu and request a rescheduled meeting (if sufficient evidence is provided to warrant the request), request a temporary hold removal, or complete the sanction(s) before the hold can be removed.

    Back to Top
  • My student has been alleged to have violated the Code of Conduct, what can I do to help?

    Encourage your student to review the Code of Conduct prior to their scheduled meeting with his/her assigned Hearing Officer. Remind your student to be respectful of the conduct process, and support your student throughout the process.

    Back to Top
  • Are parents/guardians notified if their student receives a conduct violation?

    The Office of Student Conduct and Community Standards sends all correspondence via email to the students AAMU email account. Parents or guardians may be notified when a student is found responsible for a Code of Conduct violation related to alcohol or other drugs when he/she is under the age of 21 or if the student is in violation of the University weapons policy.

    Back to Top
  • I called the main telephone number (256) 372-5233, but no one answered. What should I do?

    If you don't get an answer when you call ext. 5233 or 1147, we ask that you leave a voicemail with the following information: (1) your full name, (2) your A#, (3) a callback number, and, (4) the reason for your call. However, the best way to contact someone in the office is at conductofficer@aamu.edu.

    Back to Top
  • What are common sanctions?

    Sanctions are assigned at the discretion of the Hearng Officer or Conduct Board. Examples of sanctions outlined in the Code of Conduct include warning, probation, educational sanctions (papers, letters of apology, and/or alcohol or drug education), restitution, counseling, community service, suspension and expulsion. This list is not exhaustive, and sanctions are assigned to meet the individual needs of a student.

    Back to Top
  • What is the purpose of sanctions?

    Sanctions proposed by the Office of Student Conduct and Community Standards staff are intended to be educational and to help your student learn from the incident.

    Back to Top
  • My student was suspended; what does my student have to do before returning to the University?

    Your student needs to make a written request to the Vice President of Student Affairs asking to be allowed to return to the University via conductofficer@aamu.edu as well as the completion of any required sanctions.

    The request should be submitted 30 days prior to the student's return semester. It is the Vice President for Student Affairs' discretion whether re-admittance is allowed/permitted.

    Back to Top
  • Will anything be noted on my student’s record?

    Any violation of the Code of Conduct is noted on the student’s disciplinary record. The disciplinary record is a separate record from the academic record; violations are not noted on a student’s transcript or diploma, unless the student is dismissed from the University. However, many graduate or professional schools and some employers may request a summary of the disciplinary record.

    Back to Top
  • My student’s conduct case was dismissed; can my student’s record be cleared in the Office of Student Conduct and Community Standards?

    The University’s conduct process is educational, remedial, and administrative in nature and is separate from the court system. A student can file a request to have a minor conduct violation removed from their disciplinary record 12 months after the case is closed or thereafter by submitting the Disciplinary Record - Expungement Request Form.

    Back to Top
