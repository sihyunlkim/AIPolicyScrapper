          “Michelle Mart vividly narrates the careers and contexts of these fascinating women from the past century of American food, illustrating how their varying commitments, interventions, and passions reshaped what we eat and how we think about it.”
          —Anna Zeide, author of Canned: The Rise and Fall of Consumer Confidence in the American Food Industry

          “Chosen Women and Inka Statecraft is an important contribution to understandings of how gender and labor shaped the experiences of individuals in the Inka empire, and especially in the Inka heartland.”
          —Robyn Cutright, author of The Story of Food in the Human Past: How What We Ate Made Us Who We Are 

          “Bennett uses accounts of the experiences of three Kaqchikel Maya women to show how poverty, racism, and forced migration have led not to the abandonment of their Indigenous identities but to a newfound strength and activism in defense of their culture and communities, manifested in a preference for speaking in Maya and wearing traditional Maya clothing. A valuable case study for anthropologists, feminists, and developmentalists. Recommended.”
          —CHOICE

          “Joanne B. Mulcahy deftly weaves together the numerous strands of a complicated life. . . . While this biography could simply read as a triumph of Greenwood’s bold nature, in Mulcahy’s empathetic hands, she etches in the pitfalls of living in a man’s world.”  
          —Celia Stahr, author of Frida in America: The Creative Awakening of a Great Artist

          “Meghan Farley Webb shares the intimate stories of Maya women left behind by migrant husbands in the United States. Drawing on heartfelt conversations and in-depth observations, the author takes us into households and online posts to show how new technologies of communication both support intimacy and complicate transnational family relations. This book is a welcome study of life in highland Guatemala today.”
          —Carol Hendrickson, author of Weaving Identities: Construction of Dress and Self in a Highland Guatemala Town
